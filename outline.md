# Driving Safety

## Importance of Driving
- Driving and independence
- Goal is safe driving for as long as possible

## Why Driving Risk Changes With Age
- Vision changes
- Reaction time
- Physical mobility
- Cognitive changes
- Medication effects

## Practical Driving Habits
- Defensive driving
- Avoiding high-risk situations
- Planning routes and timing
- Adjusting driving patterns

## Vehicle Safety
- Seat belts
- Modern vehicle safety technologies
    - ABS: press the brake pedal firmly and keep steering; do not pump the brakes
    - Vehicle stability control / electronic stability control: helps correct skids, but it does not overcome speeding, tailgating, or worn tires
    - Federal requirement timing:
        - ABS was required on new passenger cars built on or after September 1, 2000, and on many light trucks / SUVs / vans under the same brake standard on or after September 1, 2002
        - Electronic stability control phased in during model years 2009-2011 and was required on all new light vehicles by model year 2012
    - Practical takeaway: if your car is roughly model year 2012 or newer, it likely has both ABS and stability control; older vehicles may have ABS only or neither feature
- Importance of understanding safety features

## Vehicle Maintenance
- Tires and tread
- Brakes
- Visibility and lighting
- Routine vehicle checks

## Health and Driving
- Vision changes and night glare
- Arthritis or limited neck mobility
- Slower reaction time
- Memory or concentration changes
- Importance of regular checkups

# Transition

## Medications and Driving
- Some medicines can affect alertness, balance, and reaction time
- Common examples include sleep aids, pain medicines, and anxiety medications
- Side effects may be worse when starting a new medicine or changing doses
- Safe driving includes knowing how your medicines affect you
- This is why medication safety is also driving safety

---

# Medication Safety

## Importance of Medication Safety
- Increasing number of medications with age
- Polypharmacy

## Common Medication Risks
- Drug interactions
- Duplicate medications
- Dosing confusion
- Side effects affecting balance or alertness

## Practical Medication Safety Habits
- Keeping an updated medication list
- Using one pharmacy when possible
- Pill organizers and reminders
- Communication with healthcare providers

## Knowing Your Medications
- Understanding purpose of medications
- Awareness of side effects
- Importance of medication review

## High-Risk Medication Categories
- Opioid pain medications
- Sleep medications
- Anxiety medications
- Risks related to falls, confusion, and driving

## Talking With Your Doctor
- Discussing medication risks
- Reviewing long-term medications
- Considering alternatives when appropriate
