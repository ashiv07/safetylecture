## slide 11
    - Polypharmacy simply means taking multiple medicines at the same time. For many older adults, medicines are added over the years by different doctors. The problem is not always one bad medicine — the problem can be too many medicines or the wrong combination. As we age, our bodies handle medicines differently, so side effects become more common. That’s why reviewing medications regularly and simplifying the list when possible can make people safer and help them stay independent.Don't get me wrong if you have a heart problem, diabetes, stroke, kidney problems all those medication's add up and they are very helpful and if needed, you definitely should not consider stopping. If you need them, you need them. But go over your medications with your physician every visitvisit and see how you can cut down.  for example.If you don't have stomach ulcer you don't need to be on Protonix or omeprazole. If you have just mild sniffles every spring, you don't need to be on antihistamine or allergy pill all year long. They may seem like benign medication's but they interact with other medications and can have serious side effects of their own.
